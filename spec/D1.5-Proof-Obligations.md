## T1 — Decidability of Typing and Evaluation

**Discharges:** D0 Assumption 4 (static analyzability); D1.2 metatheorem.

$$
\forall\, \Gamma\, e.\ \ \mathrm{Decidable}\big(\exists \tau.\ \Gamma \vdash e : \tau\big)
\qquad\wedge\qquad
\forall\, \rho\, e.\ \ \mathrm{Decidable}\big(\exists v.\ \rho \vdash e \Downarrow v\big)
$$

```lean
-- Idealized (relational) statement:
theorem typing_decidable (Γ : Ctx) (e : Expr) : Decidable (∃ τ, HasType Γ e τ) := by sorry
theorem eval_terminates  (ρ : Env) (e : Expr) : ∃ v, BigStep ρ e v := by sorry
```

**Status: PROVED (CI-compiled), scoped to the mechanized `Expr` fragment.** The
functional-model discharge lives in `mechanization/`: `Governance.Algebra.infer`
is a *total* structural sort-inference function, and
`Governance.Algebra.typing_decidable : Decidable (∃ t, HasType Γ e t)` decides
well-typedness (typing leg); `Governance.Algebra.eval_terminates : ∃ v, eval ρ e
= v` witnesses termination via the total evaluator (evaluation leg). The D1.2
"Uniqueness of Sorts" metatheorem is `typing_unique`. Scope note: the mechanized
`Expr` covers the 9 core productions (no `Count`/`Window` yet), so full T1 over
the entire D1.3 grammar remains to be extended; the relational `HasType`/`BigStep`
forms above are the spec idealization of the proved functional statements.

## T4 — Registry Semantic Inertness (Invariant I4)

**Discharges:** I4. For any bijective token renaming $\sigma$, verdicts are stable.

$$
\forall\, \sigma\ \text{bijective on } \mathrm{dom}(R),\ \forall\, \bar\eta.\ \
\mathrm{verdict}(\hat{K}, \bar\eta) = \mathrm{verdict}(\sigma\!\cdot\!\hat{K}, \bar\eta)
$$

```lean
theorem registry_inert (σ : Token ≃ Token) (K : KB) (tr : Trace) :
    verdict K tr = verdict (rename σ K) tr := by sorry
```

## T5 — Presentation Erasure (Invariant I5)

$$

