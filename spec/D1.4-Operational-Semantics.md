# D1.4 — Operational Semantics of $\hat{E}$

**Layer:** $\hat{E}$ · **Status:** DRAFT · **Depends:** D1.1, D1.2, D1.3

Two judgment forms are given: a **big-step** evaluation for the read-only algebra $T$
(no side effects, Invariant I1) and a **small-step** transition relation for the execution
layer $\hat{E}$ over the lifecycle alphabet $\Sigma$. $\hat{E}$ is the *sole* writer of
$\hat{K}$ (Invariant I2) and its passes are deterministic (Invariant I8).

## 1. Configurations

$$
\text{Config} \;\triangleq\; \langle \hat{K},\, \theta \rangle
\qquad
\theta : \mathrm{Inst} \rightharpoonup \Sigma
$$

where $\hat{K}$ is the append-only knowledge base (D1.1 §5) and $\theta$ is the current
lifecycle state map. An **event** is $\eta \in \mathbb{N} \times \text{TIX}$.

## 2. Big-Step Evaluation of $T$ (read-only)

Judgment $\;\rho \vdash e \Downarrow v\;$: under environment $\rho$ (registry + trace),
term $e$ evaluates to ground value $v$. No rule updates $\rho$.

$$
\frac{}{\rho \vdash v \Downarrow v}\ \textsc{(E-Val)}
\qquad
\frac{\rho \vdash e_1 \Downarrow n_1 \quad \rho \vdash e_2 \Downarrow n_2}{\rho \vdash (e_1 \oplus e_2) \Downarrow (n_1 \mathbin{\oplus} n_2)}\ \textsc{(E-Cmp)}
$$

$$
\frac{R = \rho.\mathrm{reg} \quad k \in \mathrm{dom}(R)}{\rho \vdash \texttt{reg}(k) \Downarrow R(k)}\ \textsc{(E-Lookup)}
\qquad
\frac{\rho \vdash e \Downarrow \mathsf{true}\ \text{at some } t' \preceq t_{\text{now}}}{\rho \vdash \texttt{ONCE}(e) \Downarrow \mathsf{true}}\ \textsc{(E-Once)}
$$

**Lemma 1 (Purity).** If $\rho \vdash e \Downarrow v$ then the write-effect of the derivation
is $\varnothing$ (proved by rule induction; D1.5 §T2).

## 3. Small-Step Transitions of $\hat{E}$

Judgment $\;\langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta\ } \langle \hat{K}', \theta' \rangle\;$.
$\hat{K}' \sqsupseteq \hat{K}$ always (append-only).

### 3.1 Guard Activation

$$
\frac{
  \textsf{GRD}(g,\Delta,p,D) \in \hat{K} \quad
  \rho_\eta \vdash g \Downarrow \mathsf{true} \quad
  \neg\,\mathrm{defeated}(\Delta, D, \theta)
}{
  \langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta\ } \langle \hat{K},\ \theta[\Delta \mapsto \mathsf{inforce}] \rangle
}\ \textsc{(S-Activate)}
$$

### 3.2 Defeasible Priority

$$
\frac{
  \textsf{GRD}(g_1,\Delta_1,p_1,D_1),\ \textsf{GRD}(g_2,\Delta_2,p_2,D_2) \in \hat{K} \quad
  \Delta_2 \subseteq D_1 \quad
  p_1 > p_2
}{
  \langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta\ } \langle \hat{K},\ \theta[\Delta_2 \mapsto \mathsf{suspended}] \rangle
}\ \textsc{(S-Defeat)}
$$

### 3.3 Violation

$$
\frac{
  \textsf{NRM}(b,c,a,{+},\mathsf{O}) = \nu \quad
  \theta(\nu) = \mathsf{inforce} \quad
  \rho_\eta \vdash \mathrm{performed}(b,a) \Downarrow \mathsf{false} \quad
  \mathrm{due}(\nu) \preceq t_\eta
}{
  \langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta\ } \langle \hat{K},\ \theta[\nu \mapsto \mathsf{violated}] \rangle
}\ \textsc{(S-Violate)}
$$

### 3.4 Power Exercise (state-producing, via $\hat{E}$ only)

$$
\frac{
  \textsf{PWR}(h,\omega,\varepsilon,n) \in \hat{K} \quad
  \eta = \langle n, \tau \rangle \quad
  \mathrm{match}(\omega, \eta) \quad
  \kappa' = \mathrm{apply}(\varepsilon, \tau)
}{
  \langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta\ } \langle \hat{K} \cup \{\kappa'\},\ \theta[\kappa' \mapsto \mathsf{inforce}] \rangle
}\ \textsc{(S-Exercise)}
$$

Here $\mathrm{apply}(\varepsilon,\tau)$ yields a **dated** `CLS`/`NRM` instance (cf. D0 §7.3);
it is the only rule that extends $\hat{K}$.

## 4. Big-Step Verdict

$$
\frac{\langle \hat{K}, \theta_0 \rangle \xrightarrow{\ \eta_1\ } \cdots \xrightarrow{\ \eta_m\ } \langle \hat{K}_m, \theta_m \rangle}{\hat{K} \vdash [\eta_1 \ldots \eta_m] \Uparrow \theta_m}\ \textsc{(Trace)}
$$

The **verdict** at coordinate $\langle t_{\text{text}}, t_{\text{fact}} \rangle$ is the
projection $\theta_m$ restricted to instances valid at that coordinate.

## 5. Determinism (Invariant I8)

**Theorem (Confluence / Determinism).** For fixed $\langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle$ and
event $\eta$, if $\langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\eta} c_1$ and
$\langle \hat{K}, \theta \rangle \xrightarrow{\eta} c_2$, then $c_1 = c_2$.
Rule selection is stratified by priority $p$, then — for equal priority — by a
**content key** (the canonical hash of the guard condition; `internal/evaluator.Resolve`,
exit-review M6), with the store id only as a last-resort tie-break for genuine
content-duplicates. Ties are **not** impossible (the earlier text claimed so
incorrectly): they are broken deterministically, and the content key makes the
order store-independent so two implementations converge. (Obligation D1.5 §T3.)


## 6. Reconciliation & errata (WS-E, exit-review M3/M10)

D1.4 predates Ruling 2 and the verdict contract; this section reconciles it with
the implemented machine (`internal/machine`, `internal/evaluator`) pending a full
rewrite. Where the rules above conflict, the implementation + this section govern.

- **S-Violate is obligation-only; Force is removed (A02).** The rule no longer
  carries a `Force = O` premise — every NRM is an obligation. The implemented
  guard is `Sign = "+"` ∧ `¬ performed(b,a)`; permission = absence of an NRM,
  prohibition = GRD (see `D0-AMENDMENTS` A02).
- **Previously-undefined predicates** (used in §3 without definition), as implemented:
  - `performed(b,a)` — the read-only event predicate `performed:<act>` holds in the
    Ê trace (`env.Predicates`);
  - `due(ν)` — currently **not** derived from a deadline component (NRM has none);
    the implementation treats the obligation as due at evaluation time. A deadline
    field is future work; the `deadline-shift` mutation has no persisted exemplar.
  - `defeated(Δ, D, θ)` — realized by the resolver: a higher-priority guard whose
    condition holds and that `defeats` the norm (D1.4 §3.2 / `Resolve`);
  - `ρ_η` — the read-only environment at event η: ⟨registry snapshot, bindings,
    trace⟩ (`evaluator.Environment`).
- **Rules present in the implementation but absent/again above:** `discharged` and
  suspension-lift transitions, and the **conditional** verdict (open-texture
  boundary → `conditional`, D0 §7.2). These are produced by the machine
  (`machine.evaluate`) but not yet written as inference rules here — a known spec
  gap to close in the D1.4 rewrite.
- **Verdict vocabulary & defaults** are now fixed by `D0-AMENDMENTS` A04
  ({compliant, violated, conditional, inapplicable, defeated} + unguarded-norm and
  INACTIVE/DEFEATED defaults) — the earlier "verdict = θ-projection" text is
  underspecified for an independent reimplementer.
- **Registry is versioned, not bitemporal (M10, known limitation).** `registry(token,
  version)` carries no ⟨t_text, t_fact⟩; `kernel_instance_at()` does not resolve
  registry state at historical coordinates. Verdicts computed at a past coordinate
  against a *later* registry version are therefore unspecified — a real gap for the
  retroactive-amendment scenarios the bitemporal design targets. Making the registry
  bitemporal is tracked as future work; until then, historical verdicts assume the
  current registry version.
